Vector Field Augmented Differentiable Policy Learning for Vision-Based Drone Racing

Yang Su*, Feng Yu*, Yu Hu, Xinze Niu,Linzuo Zhang,Fangyu Sun,Danping Zou
Shanghai Jiao Tong University
IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters (Accepted March 2026)
*Indicates Equal Contribution
Paper Arxiv Code Video

Abstract

Autonomous drone racing in complex environments requires agile, high-speed flight while maintaining reliable obstacle avoidance. Differentiable-physics-based policy learning has recently demonstrated high sample efficiency and remarkable performance, but applying such methods to drone racing remains difficult because gate traversal is hard to express as smooth differentiable losses. We propose DiffRacing, a vector field-augmented differentiable policy learning framework that combines differentiable loss and Attractive Vector Fields to provide continuous, stable gradient signals for balancing safety and high-speed gate traversal. We further introduce a differentiable Delta Action Model to compensate dynamics mismatch for efficient sim-to-real transfer. Extensive simulation and real-world experiments demonstrate superior sample efficiency, faster convergence, and robust flight performance.

Framework

Overview of the DiffRacing framework. The policy network takes depth and state observations as input. The core components are the differentiable simulator (allowing loss gradients to back-propagate to the network), the Attractive Vector Field module (augmenting policy gradients during training), and the Delta Action Model (trained to compensate for dynamics mismatch). Dashed lines indicate gradient flow. Orange lines represent policy-training data flow, where UAV dynamics rollout, image rendering, loss computation, and gradient backpropagation are carried out fully in simulation. Blue lines represent the Delta Action Model pipeline including data collection, model training, and policy fine-tuning.

Method

An Intuitive Illustration of Trajectory Self-Correction.

DiffRacing uses differentiable dynamics to backpropagate training loss through simulator rollouts, while Attractive Vector Fields provide a geometric prior for gate traversal and reduce local-optimum issues caused by purely loss-based objectives. During training, AVF guidance is combined with differentiable loss gradients to balance high-speed racing and safety. To improve transfer robustness, a differentiable Delta Action Model learns action-space corrections for dynamics mismatch, enabling efficient sim-to-real adaptation without explicit system identification.

Real-World Experiments

Real-world experiments. The policy is deployed on a physical drone navigating (a-c) zigzag and (d) circular tracks with complex, obstacle-dense layouts that were unseen during training. The maximum velocities recorded in (a), (b), and (c) are 5.9, 6.4, and 6.0 m/s, respectively.
